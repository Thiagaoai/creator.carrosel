## Carousel Structure Rules
- **Slide 1 (hook):** Visceral sensory line or bold statement. Ex: "The difference between good and unforgettable is heat control."
- **Middle slides:** One technique, tip, or fact per slide. Dense flavor — no filler.
- **Last slide (CTA):** Strong close. Ex: "Fire it up tonight." or "Tag someone who needs to learn this."
- Captions should feel punchy, like a pitmaster talking directly to you.
- Avoid overly polished or corporate language.

## Tone Examples
- Good: "Most people pull the steak too early. Here is why patience is the secret ingredient."
- Good: "Oak burns long. Cherry burns sweet. Mesquite burns bold. Know your wood."
- Bad: "Flamma offers high-quality grilling content for all your outdoor cooking needs."
